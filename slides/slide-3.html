< How Butteflies Navigate >

    Butterflies use physics in several ways to navigate:

    • Sun position and polarized light help them orient and maintain a flight path.
    • Magnetic sensing (magnetoreception) allows them to detect Earth's magnetic field.
    • Aerodynamics govern their flight mechanics, helping them generate lift and thrust.

    Fluid dynamics and thermal updrafts help them conserve energy and navigate efficiently over long distances.
