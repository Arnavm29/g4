< Butterfly Color Spectrum and Sensitivity >
  • Blues and Purples:
    • Flowers like lavender, butterfly bush (Buddleia), and bluebells are particularly attractive to butterflies that are sensitive to these colors. These species often have a strong preference for these flowers because the colors, along with UV patterns, signal the presence of nectar.
    • Butterfly species like the Common Blue, Peacock Butterfly, and Cabbage White are attracted to flowers with blue, purple, and violet hues.
  • Yellow and Orange:
    • Flowers such as sunflowers, goldenrod, and marigolds are favorites for butterflies that prefer yellow. The Yellow Swallowtail, Skipper butterflies, and others gravitate toward these vibrant yellow and orange flowers due to their bright color and nectar.
  • Red:
    • Some species, like the Red Admiral or Monarch Butterfly, are attracted to red flowers, like hummingbird sage or red clover. However, butterflies typically rely more on UV patterns and flower shape when visiting red flowers because their vision is less sensitive to this color.
